Section 05

Branding & Identity

Your branding details provide the visual and emotional blueprint for your new website. These assets guarantee our design team builds a consistent digital extension of your existing brand — pixel perfect, on every screen.

What Branding Covers

This section covers four elements: your logo files, brand color codes, typography, and tagline. If you don't have all of these yet, read the No Brand Yet? section below for guidance.

1. Existing Logo

Your logo is the anchor of your website. Provide the original, high-resolution source files so our developers can scale it cleanly across all device sizes without pixelation.

Format Recommended Best Used For
.SVG Preferred Vector format — scales to any size perfectly. Ideal for web headers, favicons, and retina displays.
.PNG Accepted Transparent background support. Minimum 1000px wide. Good for web use when vector is unavailable.
.AI / .EPS Accepted Original vector source files from Adobe Illustrator. Ideal for future rebranding or print use.
.JPG / .JPEG Avoid No transparency support. Compressed quality. Only use if no other format is available.
Provide Both Versions If Possible

If you have both a full-color logo and a white/dark monochrome version, provide both. We use different logo versions for different backgrounds — dark navbars, light hero sections, email footers, and favicon generation.

Brand Colors

2. Brand Colors

Provide specific color codes (Hex codes) to ensure your precise corporate palette is programmed into your website's buttons, headers, and backgrounds consistently across every page.

Color Code Format 
Primary Color:      #FFD700   (Gold — URHIDU brand example)
Secondary Color:    #111111   (Near black)
Accent Color:       #F8F9FA   (Off white / Surface)
Background Color:   #FFFFFF   (Pure white)
Text Color:         #1A1A1A   (Dark soft)

How Colors Map to Your Website

Color Role Where It Appears on Your Website
Primary Main call-to-action buttons, links, active navigation highlights, key section accents
Secondary Header background, footer, dark section backgrounds, secondary buttons
Accent Card backgrounds, hover states, subtle section separators, input field fills
Background Main page background, content containers, modal overlays
Text All body copy, headings, labels, captions across the entire site
Hex Codes Only, Please

Do not submit color names like "navy blue" or "dark green." These are subjective and vary across monitors. Always provide the exact 6-character Hex code (e.g., #003366) so we can program your precise shade with zero ambiguity.

Typography

3. Typography (Fonts)

List your official typography names so we can license and embed matching web fonts. Typography is one of the most powerful tools for expressing your brand's personality and professionalism.

Heading / Display Font

If Available

The bold, eye-catching typeface used for page titles, hero headings, and section labels. This font defines your brand's visual personality at first glance.

Example: Poppins Bold, Playfair Display, Montserrat ExtraBold

Body / Paragraph Font

If Available

The readable typeface used for all body text, descriptions, menus, and form fields. This font must be highly legible across all screen sizes.

Example: Lato Regular, Open Sans, Source Sans Pro

Don't Know Your Font Names?

If you see a font you love on a website, tools like WhatFont (a free browser plugin) can identify the exact font name. Alternatively, share a screenshot of your print materials or existing brand assets and our team will identify the typeface for you.

Tagline

4. Tagline / Brand Slogan

Supply your core business slogan so we can position it prominently inside your website's main banner area. A great tagline tells visitors exactly who you are and what you stand for in one powerful line.

No Brand Yet?

No Logo or Brand Yet?

If you don't have a logo or established brand colors yet, that is completely fine. URHIDU Company offers complete brand identity creation as part of your website development package.

1

Submit What You Have

If you have social media profiles, business cards, or any existing visual material — share links or files with our team. This gives our designers a starting reference point for your current visual direction.

2

Brand Identity Discovery Session

Our creative team will schedule a brief discovery call to understand your brand personality, industry, target audience, and preferences before designing options.

3

Logo & Color Palette Delivery

We deliver a custom logo with a complete color palette and typography pairing recommendation. Once approved, these assets are immediately applied to your website build.
